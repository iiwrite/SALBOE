Cycle

Photo by Justyn Warner on Unsplash

I sit and stare at a blank-lit screen The vertical line blinks at a metronome's beat Almost like rhythm of my heart.

I wait to write that perfect line A line, that makes buried wound bleed A line, that awakens rivers long run dry A line, that will survive the test of time.

Ideas swirl like autumn leaves in a gust I hunt them down and snatch one Glue it onto the canvas Only to peel it off.

Ideas swirl again like autumn leaves in a gust I glue one onto the canvas, again, and again Only to erase it clean again.

Time slips by, day turns into night The vertical line still blinks at a metronome's beat I turn away from the screen. Until it slowly glows, dims, and dissolves in darkness.
